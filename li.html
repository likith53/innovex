Welcome to Hampi Heritage

Discover the magnificent ruins of Hampi, Karnataka - a cultural and architectural heritage site built more than 200 years ago. Once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, this UNESCO World Heritage Site showcases stunning temples, ancient monuments, and architectural marvels that tell the story of India's glorious past.

Explore the architectural treasures of Hampi, Karnataka.

Virupaksha Temple

The principal temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, featuring a magnificent 9-tiered gopuram tower and intricate carvings that showcase the architectural brilliance of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Vittala Temple Complex

Famous for its exceptional architecture and the iconic stone chariot. The musical pillars of this temple produce different musical notes when tapped, demonstrating ancient engineering genius.

Lotus Mahal

An exquisite palatial structure in the Zenana enclosure, combining Indo-Islamic architectural styles with its unique lotus-shaped domed pavilion.
